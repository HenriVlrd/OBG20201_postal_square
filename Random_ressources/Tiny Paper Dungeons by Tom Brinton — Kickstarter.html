    L'histoire


    Hi, I'm Tom Brinton.

    I'm creating a procedurally-generated pencil and paper dungeon that you can easily carry in the back pocket of your jeans. 


    The basics:

    • Each book is unique, because:
    • Each page is randomly generated
    • Play wherever, whenever

    What you get:

    A 3x5" inch memo pad printed in Provo, UT. It has a thick front and back cover. Includes a matte black golf pencil that slides perfectly into the spiral binding, and a black and white d6 that you can carry along as well.




    How to play:

    Gameplay is simple but entertaining: you roll a 6-sided die to determine how far you'll move each turn. Then pick a direction, draw a line, and interact with anything on that line, be it a monster (ouch!) some treasure (nice!) or a teleporter (cool!).

    Customize your character, roll your way through each floor, and get items at the shops sprinkled throughout the game. There's even a page in the back to track stats like how many times you died -- so if you die, don't worry! It's all part of the adventure.



    How I'm making it:

    I am using software called Drawbot that allows me to code a level generator in Python. This generates PDFs which I will send to a local printer to get the notebooks made. This means that each game pad is different - each one has random level layouts and different shops. So if you get more than one game pad, you'll have different levels to play!




    Why I'm making it:

    I feel compelled to cut games down to their bones and to see what makes them tick. In that spirit, I'm trying to strip this genre down to its simplest form: 1 player, simple mechanics, no story-telling, just you, a die and pencil, and a series of simple decisions in the face of randomness. 


    As a product designer, I have been thinking for a long time about the concept of "paper apps". In fact I've created one already. Sometimes you want to do something during a dull moment, but don't want to get sucked into your phone screen. Paper apps fill that gap with discrete, screen-free experiences that respect your time and attention. Eventually I plan on creating a whole Paper App Library (PAL) that will include all sorts of little notebook "apps", including games and utilities.

    Risques et défis

    The main risk involves getting printed and shipped. However, I have made "paper apps" like this before and I have a process in place. COVID restrictions may slow things down, but only by a week or two. I have factored all of this into the delivery times.
